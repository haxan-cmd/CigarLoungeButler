# Chivalry 2 Lore

Curated from the game's in-client lore pages. This is the Butler's canonical
reference for world and character lore. One faction per top-level section, one
entry per sub-heading (keeps it parseable for context injection).

Note the tie-in: Arch Precept Gyeoff's "hundred hands" that deliver his words
throughout the kingdom is the in-lore root of the Hundred-Handed feat.

---

## Agatha (Agathian Knights)

### World — Conflict Summary
The Agathian Knights were created when King Argon I, king of Olde Agatha,
conquered the disparate kingdoms in the Unification War. Their leadership is
dominated by the nobility of Agatha, especially well-to-do families from the
rich provinces. They are highly religious with a strong sense of birthright,
loyalty, and honour. Their love of justice, coin and beauty leads them to
greatness and folly.

Agatha, led by the Steward King Feydrid Kearn, was utterly crushed by the Mason
Order at the end of The Civil War (Chivalry 1) and now returns under the
leadership of Argon II.

Having suffered a recent defeat to a ruthless enemy, there is a growing yet not
uniform desire to cast aside the mercies once shown by their kind.

### King Argon I (Deceased)
The grand unifier, King Argon the First, by his steel and charisma, with his
hardy warriors of the north, made all other rulers bend the knee and recognize
his sovereignty over the realms. Yet his ambition would be his undoing: soon
after unifying the lands he grew restless with the peace and declared a crusade
upon the strange heretical lands to the south. Argon fell bravely in combat in
those harsh desert sands, leaving behind a great kingdom as his legacy.

### King Argon II
Emerging from the distant frozen reaches of Olde Agatha with youthful ferocity
and an appetite for battle, Argon II takes his seat in Thayic Castle as sovereign
of Olde Agatha with the blessing of the Church and the Agathian Royal Council.
From here, twenty years after Malric stole the crown, King Argon II begins his
campaign of reconquest. The heathens who support the false king Malric shall be
brought to heel or be put to the blade, as god wills it.

Argon II's origins are as much legend as history. He claims to have fled as a
child from an outpost nearby Stoneshill under cover of night just before the
infamous assault. His personal guardian and combat tutor, Kosmas Ironkiln,
escorted the boy through the wilderness for weeks until the pair took passage on
a fishing vessel under assumed names, ultimately reaching the shores of
Galencourt where he was raised and taught in the arts of war and god by the monks
led by Arch Precept Gyeoff.

The time finally came to reveal himself to the world just as the kingdom was
pushing closer to the edge of war. In the heartlands of Olde Agatha, ancestral
homeland of Argon's family, he made his identity known to the Agathian Council,
who made no hesitation to recognize his claim and offer their benevolent guidance
to this birthright king of Agatha.

Those who oppose Argon II questioned the convenience of his arrival at the edge of
war, and thus the legitimacy of his claim to the throne.

### Feydrid Kearn (Deceased)
Former steward king of Agatha, Feydrid took up leadership of the loyalists after
Argon I fell in battle. His valiant yet ultimately futile defense at the battle
of Stoneshill marked the end of the first civil war and led to 20 years of Mason
rule. Unresolved rumours that Feydrid intended to remain on the throne
indefinitely followed him to his grave.

### Barek Thorne
A renowned commander and warrior who fought alongside Feydrid during the first
civil war, Barek Thorne was captured and taken prisoner shortly after Feydrid's
death. For 20 years he was tortured and displayed, enslaved as a symbol to those
who would dare claim their own strength as equal to Malric. For 20 years he
endured punishments and spat in the face of the Order from his chains in Falmire.

### Arch Precept Gyeoff
The holy personification of the Temple of Light, Arch Precept Gyeoff preaches
peacefulness in contrast to the constant bloodshed and battle the people of Agatha
engage in, preferring instead that people should follow the noble example set
forth by the grazing animals. Others claim his influence as a driving force in
sanctioning the newfound brutality of the Agathian Knights.

The Arch Precept presides over the holy monastery at Galencourt; from there his
hundred hands deliver his words throughout the kingdom.

### Agathian Royal Council
The Agathian Royal Council is formed of seven nobles who represented the remaining
power structure of Agatha when Feydrid fell. Seeing the cause was lost, they
jointly surrendered to Malric and the Mason Order in exchange for the preservation
of Galencourt, return of Feydrid's corpse, and other regional provisions.
Following increasing unrest from the nobility under Mason rule, the council was
reformed and preparations for war were already underway when Argon II arrived to
lead the effort.

Members of the council include Kosmas Ironkiln (the mentor of Argon II), Edward
Kearn (Feydrid's uncle), the Grand Merchant of Parodin, the Great Lady of
Arathane, the Lord of Irilla, and the hand of the Church.

## Mason Order

### World — Overview
The Mason Order was founded by the rebellious General Malric in the wake of
Argon I's death during The Crusade. The forming of the Order led to the First
Civil War, from which, after much bloodshed and grueling siege, the Masons
emerged victorious. After securing the remainder of the Kingdom under his rule,
Malric set out to replenish the strength of his army and leave no question as to
the legitimacy of his rule.

The core Mason values of strength, discipline and ferocity are the driving force
in everything they do. The embarrassment of defeat to the foreign Tenosians
birthed the desire in Malric to reforge the lands and people of Agatha to be
harder and stronger. The commoners most afflicted by his wrath are his most
ardent supporters, for those among them who consider themselves strong would pay
any price to be given the opportunity to rise above the crushing limits of
birthright and piety.

### King Malric
Malric, the Iron-Fist, founder of the Mason Order, King of all the realms once
unified beneath the banner of Argon I. Before he sat upon the throne, Malric was
Argon I's right-hand man and leading commander; his exceptional strategic mind
and warrior prowess led him to fame in the kingdom. As ruthless as he was
cunning, Malric inspired a potent mix of fear and admiration in those who would
strive to prove themselves worthy of his respect, though perhaps some merely
sought to avoid his wrath.

As King, Malric's focus was always directed to hardening the Kingdom, to
bulwarking against inevitable retaliation from Tenosia, and to shutting down
dissent by any means necessary.

Malric had little patience for fools, being known for his short and furious
temper. Even so, some claim a softer side was once known to him, before he was
twisted by the cruelties of his fate. (Agatha calls him "the false king Malric.")

### Mason Heir — Valen Tray
Malric's bastard son, born of Argon's daughter. Born out of wedlock, he was given
the local Vantear bastard name "Tray". From a young age, Valen received the
highest-caliber training in the arts of war and governance from a host of the
greatest blademasters and strategists in the kingdom. His unending intensity in
his studies was remarkable even to his teachers; they say he seemed driven by an
unwavering desire to match the greatness of his father. Although the kingdom will
one day be his, he has been given strict instruction to stay well within the
shadow of his father, lest he draw Malric's ire. (Note: through his mother Soree,
Valen carries Argon I's blood — the Mason heir is Argon I's grandson.)

### Soree Argon (Deceased)
The daughter of King Argon I and lover of Malric, Soree Argon became pregnant with
Malric's child just as the forces marched off to The Crusade. Shortly after the
night her son Valen was born, Soree learned of her father's death. The childbirth
and anguish were too much for the new mother to bear, and she fell into an illness
that would claim her life mere days before she and Malric would have reunited.

Or so that's the tale as Malric recalls it. If he and Soree were indeed lovers it
was a most secretive affair, but of course it would have had to be, since Malric
lacked the noble pedigree of the Argons.

### Olar the Wise
Not all strength is measured in brawn. Olar's physical form is as pathetic as his
mind is impressive. Like any true genius, he is stricken by occasional bouts of
madness, and has an odd and off-putting fascination with rodents. Some suggest he
is not fond of the Order and that he may even have been a slave forced to use his
mind against his will.

### The High Forge
The Mason Council, better known as The High Forge, gathered in Trayan Citadel
along with King Malric. This elite group are among the few granted direct access
to Malric and worked together to achieve his vision for the kingdom and reforge
the realm with brutal efficiency. Members included the Warlord of Vantear, the
First Maiden of Marinar, the Great Reaper of Ramnor, the Prime of Barema, and a
man known simply as The Beak, a crooked old advisor who whispers in Malric's ear.

## Tenosia (Tenosian Empire)

### World — Overview
Tenosia, the strange lands to the south, have long hovered as a distant threat
over the lands of Agatha after the failed crusade that led to the death of King
Argon the 1st and ultimately ignited the civil war between the Mason Order and
Agathian Knights.

Now Tenosia strikes at Baudwyn's Bulwark, the longstanding barrier that has held
these empires apart and stood unconquered for centuries. Rumours of devastating
new technology threaten this ancient stronghold, and if it falls, the lands of
Agatha shall burn and weep beneath the vengeance of the Tenosian Empire.

The empire is a sophisticated and academic culture which had enjoyed a long peace
prior to the invasion of the first Crusades. That all changed when war was brought
to their shores by the barbarians to the north, and they have spent the last 20
years plotting their retaliation. Built mostly on harsh, arid lands, their people
have adapted with wondrous engineering, city planning and star-gazing that leave
their lands prosperous and rich.

### Myah Vane
Following the chaos in Tenosia at the outset of the war, Myah Vane rose to power
after a series of ruthless assassinations of her own family who sat closer in line
to the throne. While most of the nobility were content to cheer their victory in
defending against the crusades, she knew how lucky they were that the invaders had
so underestimated the harsh climate.

Their people had been allowed to grow fat and soft, and the great wealth of the
empire was vulnerable. Through a unique mixture of ruthlessness and cunning, she
convinced the nobility, often by example, that they could no longer afford to put
aside the duty of war to the empire. The Tigress of Tenosia built her name and
bolstered the imperial army on strong claims that she would not rest until the
lands of the North were hers or hells.

### Tahar Rahman
Tahar Rahman, Master of the Curved Blades and leader of the imperial army, is
considered the greatest warrior in Tenosia. He claims to have slain King Argon the
1st himself in single combat, and has become a beloved champion to the Tenosian
people. A rugged man of little words and swift action, he stands as the Queen's
right-hand man and her direct link to the military support of the empire.

## World Map

The known world spans several regions, most of which surface as the community's
map/faction names:

- **Olde Agatha** — the frozen northern heartland, ancestral home of the Argons
  and seat of Thayic Castle. Contains Thayic, Falmire's neighbourhood, and the
  Agathian core (Stoneshill, Galencourt).
- **Parodin** — central province; home of the Grand Merchant of Parodin (Agathian
  Council). Contains Coxwell and Wardenglade.
- **Arathane** — eastern highlands; the Great Lady of Arathane sits on the Agathian
  Council. Contains Dark Forest (Darkforest).
- **Ramnor** — western reach; the Great Reaper of Ramnor sits on the High Forge.
  Contains Falmire.
- **Irilla** — south-central; the Lord of Irilla sits on the Agathian Council.
  Contains Lionspire and the Tournament Grounds.
- **Marinar** — southwestern coast; the First Maiden of Marinar sits on the High
  Forge.
- **Barema** — southern lands; the Prime of Barema sits on the High Forge. Contains
  the Zelten Fighting Pit.
- **Vantear** — far eastern frontier; the Warlord of Vantear sits on the High
  Forge, and Valen "Tray" carries a Vantear bastard name. Contains Rudhelm and
  Cita City.

### Map / lore cross-references (for connecting a played map to its lore)
- **Trayan Citadel** — where the High Forge gathered with Malric → the map
  "Regicide at Trayan Citadel."
- **Baudwyn's Bulwark** — the ancient barrier Tenosia now assaults → the map
  "The Breach of Baudwyn."
- **Galencourt** — Arch Precept Gyeoff's holy monastery, where Argon II was raised.
- **Falmire** — where Barek Thorne was chained for 20 years.
- **Stoneshill** — where Feydrid Kearn fell, ending the first civil war.
- **Coxwell, Lionspire, Rudhelm, Aberfell, Askandir, Bridgetown, Montcrux,
  Darkforest** — battlefields across these regions.

### The through-line (for quick reference)
Argon I unified the realms, then died on a failed southern crusade (Tahar Rahman
of Tenosia claims the kill). His commander Malric founded the Mason Order, won the
First Civil War against the loyalists under Feydrid Kearn (who fell at Stoneshill),
and ruled for 20 years. Argon I's daughter Soree bore Malric a son, Valen Tray —
so the Mason heir is Argon's own grandson. Now Argon II returns from hiding to
reclaim Agatha from "the false king Malric," while Tenosia, humiliated by the old
crusade, strikes north at Baudwyn's Bulwark under Queen Myah Vane and her champion
Tahar Rahman. Three-way war.
